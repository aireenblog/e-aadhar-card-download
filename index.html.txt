    <h1>How to Download e Aadhaar Card Online – Easy Steps Explained</h1>

    <p>In today’s digital-first world, having access to your official documents anytime, anywhere is important. One such essential document is your <strong>Aadhaar card</strong>. Thankfully, the <strong>e Aadhaar card download</strong> process is straightforward, free, and available to everyone through the <strong>UIDAI website</strong>.</p>

    <p>Whether you’ve lost your physical card, need a digital version for KYC, or want to store it safely on your mobile, this guide explains <strong>how to download e Aadhaar</strong> using different methods — by Aadhaar number, enrollment ID, or even name and date of birth.</p>

    <h2>What is an e Aadhaar Card?</h2>
    <p>An <strong>e Aadhaar</strong> is the digital version of your Aadhaar card issued by UIDAI (Unique Identification Authority of India). It’s <strong>digitally signed, password-protected</strong>, and just as valid as the physical Aadhaar.</p>

    <ul>
      <li>Online KYC (Know Your Customer) processes</li>
      <li>Mobile verification</li>
      <li>Income tax filing</li>
      <li>Government scheme applications</li>
    </ul>

    <h2>1. How to Download e Aadhaar by Aadhaar Number</h2>
    <p><strong>Steps:</strong></p>
    <ol>
      <li>Visit the official website: <a href="https://eaadhaar.uidai.gov.in/" target="_blank">https://eaadhaar.uidai.gov.in/</a></li>
      <li>Select the option: “Aadhaar”</li>
      <li>Enter your <strong>12-digit Aadhaar number</strong></li>
      <li>Enter the security captcha</li>
      <li>Click on <strong>“Send OTP”</strong></li>
      <li>You’ll receive a one-time password on your registered mobile number</li>
      <li>Enter OTP and verify</li>
      <li>Click “Download Aadhaar”</li>
    </ol>
    <p><strong>Tip:</strong> Make sure your mobile number is linked to your Aadhaar for OTP verification.</p>

    <h2>2. Download e Aadhaar by Enrollment ID (EID)</h2>
    <ol>
      <li>Go to <a href="https://eaadhaar.uidai.gov.in/" target="_blank">UIDAI’s download portal</a></li>
      <li>Select “Enrollment ID (EID)”</li>
      <li>Enter your 14-digit EID and 14-digit date/time (from your slip)</li>
      <li>Fill the security code and click <strong>“Send OTP”</strong></li>
      <li>Enter the OTP and click “Download Aadhaar”</li>
    </ol>

    <h2>3. Download e Aadhaar by Name and Date of Birth</h2>
    <p><strong>Steps:</strong></p>
    <ol>
      <li>Visit: <a href="https://resident.uidai.gov.in/find-uid-eid" target="_blank">https://resident.uidai.gov.in/find-uid-eid</a></li>
      <li>Choose either “Aadhaar No (UID)” or “Enrollment No (EID)”</li>
      <li>Enter your full name and date of birth</li>
      <li>Enter mobile number or email (linked to Aadhaar)</li>
      <li>Submit to receive your Aadhaar number via SMS</li>
      <li>Then, go back to the main UIDAI site and follow the download steps</li>
    </ol>
    <p><strong>Note:</strong> This only works if your mobile number/email is already linked with Aadhaar.</p>

    <h2>4. Download Aadhaar Using the mAadhaar App</h2>
    <ol>
      <li>Install the mAadhaar app (available on Android & iOS)</li>
      <li>Register using your mobile number</li>
      <li>Add your Aadhaar profile</li>
      <li>Go to the “Download Aadhaar” section and follow prompts</li>
    </ol>
    <p><strong>Issue:</strong> If <em>mAadhaar OTP not received</em>, try again or check your mobile network.</p>

    <h2>What is the Password to Open Aadhaar PDF?</h2>
    <p>The file is password-protected for security.</p>
    <p><strong>Password format:</strong> First 4 letters of your name in <strong>CAPITALS</strong> + birth year</p>
    <p><strong>Example:</strong> RAVI1990</p>

    <h2>People Also Ask (PAA)</h2>

    <h3>Is e Aadhaar valid as original?</h3>
    <p><strong>Yes.</strong> The e Aadhaar is digitally signed and valid for all official purposes.</p>

    <h3>Can I download Aadhaar if my mobile number is not registered?</h3>
    <p><strong>No.</strong> You must update your mobile number to receive OTP.</p>
    <p>Visit the nearest Aadhaar Seva Kendra to link your number.</p>

    <h3>What to do if I don’t receive OTP?</h3>
    <ul>
      <li>Check network signal</li>
      <li>Try again after 1-2 minutes</li>
      <li>Switch browsers if needed</li>
    </ul>
